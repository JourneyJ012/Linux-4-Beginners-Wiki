# Getting Started

So, you want to take the dip, huh? Well, let's go.

## Is it compatible with my daily life?

Currently, this is a big fat *maybe.* 

If all of your work is done in the browser, you will be fine.

For the person who has to process word and excel documents daily, making complex formulae and whatnot, the lack of modern releases of Microsoft Office may be your downfall. If you wish to check out if this is the case, you can download [LibreOffice](https://www.libreoffice.org/download/download-libreoffice/) and attempt to use it in your daily life.

For the 3D Modeller, we have Blender, Autodesk Maya and Mudbox, alongside Adobe Substance 3D Painter, Modeler, and Designer. Unfortunately, we don't have 3DS Max or Zbrush. CAD is also a weakpoint for Linux. FreeCAD is the only major software available.

For the Gamer, you are most likely* fine. Few games don't support Linux, although most of them are large titles. Fortnite, League of Legends, Valorant, Call of Duty, among other titles, do not support Linux. If you wish to see a list, [AreWeAntiCheat](https://areweanticheatyet.com/) (AWAC) maintain a list of supported and unsupported online games, whilst [ProtonDB](https://www.protondb.com/) focus on Steam games in general. AWAC is the first place to look for online games, whilst almost everything else is on ProtonDB.
