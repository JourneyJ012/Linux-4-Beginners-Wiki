## Deciding your Linux Distribution
This is one of the final steps before switching. You've read up on what you're getting into, and now you're nearly here.

A distribution of Linux is basically its flavour. Anyone with enough skill can make their own distribution, and they are typically powered by donations. This Wiki is primarily focused on two Linux distributions, Linux Mint and the Fedora Project's Fedora Linux. If you wish to, you can find one that is more to your liking, but this Wiki will not provide full support for distributions other than Mint or Fedora.

### Differences between Mint and Fedora
A table is provided at the end with a summary.

Linux Mint is a stable & user-friendly focused Linux version that has slightly dated (typically <3 months or 1 version behind) software. It is beginner focused, having a large variety of applications to assist the user in getting help without touching the terminal. Updates to the operating system happen a few times per year, and don't change anything major (typically adding more hardware support, updates to their own apps, and the like).

Fedora Linux is a lot more cutting-edge, having the latest versions of most Linux software, but it is a bit less focused on beginners. Operating System updates happen twice a year and pretty much **require** the usage of the terminal, although only for around 2 commands. Fedora is super customisable, and is able to feel like a Windows device, a Mac device, or anything inbetween, whilst Mint is closer to Windows.

| Fedora                        | Mint                                 |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **Brand new software**        | typically 0-1 versions behind        |
| May require the terminal more | **Probably won't need the terminal** |
| Less focused on beginners     | **More focused on beginners**        |
| More customisable             | More windows-themed                  |
| 2 releases per year           | Smaller releases per year            |
